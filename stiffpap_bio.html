Bio

Stiff Pap is a Cape Town based electronic music duo fusing elements of kwaito, gqom, house and hip hop music to create their own unique sound. The duo consists of rapper AyemaProbllem and electronic music producer Jakinda.

Established in 2016, the duo describes their sound as Industrial kwaito - a dark intoxicating sound comparable to U.S. punk/hip-hop band Death Grips. With Ayema’s enticing raps and Jakinda’s avant-garde production, the duo is at the cutting edge of African electronic music.

In 2017 The duo received major national attention for their explosive live performances, which helped them win Africa’s inaugural Afropunk Battle of The Bands, earning them a place on the world renowned Afropunk Music Festival main stage. Within less than two years in the music scene, the duo has performed at some of South Africa’s biggest music festivals including Afropunk Johannesburg, Red Bull Music Festival and Cape Town Electronic Music Festival. Their combination of music, dance, fashion and film in their live shows make them, arguably, one of the most exciting live acts in South Africa.

Since releasing their debut single ‘Dlala’ via Red Bull Studios on the 21st of October 2016, the duo has caught the eye of various media publications both locally and internationally including: Okayafrica, Afropunk, Boiler Room and i-D (Vice). Dlala was eventually chosen as one of the top 5 South African songs of 2016 by Red Bull Studios Cape Town. The song was also selected as the theme song for the Red Bull Culture Clash, one of Red Bull’s most significant cultural events globally, hosted in Johannesburg at the Orlando Stadium on the 23rd of September 2017.

In 2017, the duo released their highly anticipated debut EP, Based On a Qho Story via Red Bull Studios on the 7th of June, which was followed by positive reviews both locally and internationally. Notable reviews include: Platform, SlikourOnLife and Bubblegum Club, with an international premiere via Okayafrica. In December 2017, the EP was chosen as one of the top 30 South African Hip Hop albums of the year by Okayafrica, bringing the group to the forefront of South Africa’s finest Hip Hop artists. With a highly anticipated new project in the works (including music videos) and a growing fan base, it is safe to say that the duo is forging their own path into the future of South African electronic music.
